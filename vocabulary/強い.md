## Mnemonics:
海流はとても強く、その猫を川まで運んだ。
* The ocean's currents were too strong for the cat and it was carried away.
* This is a single kanji with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [い](https://jisho.org/search/い)</span> on the end, meaning you know it's probably an adjective. What's the adjective form of <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> strong</span>? It's also <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> strong</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: <br />So you wanna be <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> strong</span> huh? Well, all you have to do is eat these <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> two yo</span>gurts (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [つよ](https://jisho.org/search/つよ)</span>). One is filled with horse steroids, the other is full of bull steroids. They will make you so strong.
