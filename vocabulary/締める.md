## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> tighten</span> so the verb vocab version means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to tighten</span>.
* You go <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to tighten</span> and <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to tie</span> your captive up, and you realize you don't have anything to do it with. Then you see a sheep standing nearby. Okay. You take the <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> shee</span>p (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [し](https://jisho.org/search/し)</span>), pull its wool, and tie the sheep and your captive up together around a tree. Problem solved!
