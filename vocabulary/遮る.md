## Mnemonics:

* 
* This word consists of kanji with hiragana attached. Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> intercept</span>, so the verb version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to intercept</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to interrupt</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading.  You haven't learned this reading yet, so here's a mnemonic to help you:<br />The thing you want <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to interrupt</span> and <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to intercept</span> is your roommate's <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> sighing gui</span>tar (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [さえぎ](https://jisho.org/search/さえぎ)</span>). He keeps playing it and all it does is make these horrible, long sighing sounds. You want to interrupt it any cost.
