| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> consult</span> and the verb vocab version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to consult with</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to discuss</span>.
* You need <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to consult with</span> a <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> hacker</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [はか](https://jisho.org/search/はか)</span>) to solve your hacking problem. Imagine consulting with a hacker, whatever a hacker looks like in your mind.
