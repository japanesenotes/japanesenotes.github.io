# <bigfont> 流れる</bigfont> - To Stream, To Flow 

| Vocabulary | Meanings | Reading | Characters | Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 流れる | To Stream, To Flow | ながれる |  [流](../kanjis/流.md) (Stream) | [Level 14](../levels/wk_level14.md) |

Previous vocab: [気持ち悪い](気持ち悪い.md) Next vocab: [動かす](動かす.md) 

## Mnemonics:
流れる水の音が好きなので、涙が君の頬を流れるままにしておいてくれよ。ほら、とても美しいよ。
* I like the sound of flowing water, so just let your tears stream down your cheeks. See? It’s so beautiful.
* This word consists of kanji with hiragana attached. Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> stream</span>? It's <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to stream</span> (though it's also <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to flow</span>, a very similar word). You can differentiate this from <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [流す](https://jisho.org/search/流す)</span> because with <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [流す](https://jisho.org/search/流す)</span> you flush something down the toilet, which causes it to overflow so you <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> sue</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [す](https://jisho.org/search/す)</span>) the toilet maker. With <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [流れる](https://jisho.org/search/流れる)</span> you find a <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> rare</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [れる](https://jisho.org/search/れる)</span>) jewel trying to flow down the river. You grab it, and see it's a rare diamond!
* The reading is the same as <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [流す](https://jisho.org/search/流す)</span>. So long as you know that you should be able to read this one as well!
