## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> dry</span>. This verb vocab version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to get dry</span>. You're not doing the drying, it's just happening. That's <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> coo</span>', you say, things can get dry.
* The <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> 川</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [かわ](https://jisho.org/search/かわ)</span>) has started <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to get dry</span>. Need to use less water around here otherwise it'll become completely dry.
