## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> sink</span> so the verb vocab version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to sink</span>. This is when you're doing the sinking.
* When you sink something, like a battleship, you go on and say: <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> she's</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [しず](https://jisho.org/search/しず)</span>) sunk, I sunk her. That's like your tagline, or something. You say it for some reason because you think it's cool.
