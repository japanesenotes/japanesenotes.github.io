## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> decorate</span>. The verb vocab version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to decorate</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> To decorate</span> something, you don't really need a <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> cause</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [かざ](https://jisho.org/search/かざ)</span>). You should just do it, just be<span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> cause</span>.
