## Mnemonics:
絶望のどん底に落ちて、苦しい思いをしている、そこのあなた！もしや、銀行があなたの家を差し押さえちゃったとか？もし、そうなら、我々はあなたのためにとっておきのものをご用意しております…その名も「インスタント・アルツハイマー」！我々のこの新薬を使えば、あなたは辛い思い出を忘ることができますよ！
* Are you suffering from a broken heart? Has the bank recently foreclosed on your house? If so, we have just the thing for you... Instant Alzheimer’s. Our new medication will allow you to put your woes behind you.
* This is a single kanji with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [い](https://jisho.org/search/い)</span> on the end, meaning you know it's probably an adjective. What's the adjective form of <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> suffering</span>? It's a description of that word, making it <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> painful</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: <br />Everything is really <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> painful</span> right now. Why? You have something heavy on top of you, <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> cru</span>shing (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [くる](https://jisho.org/search/くる)</span>) you.
