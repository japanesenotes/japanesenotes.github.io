## Mnemonics:

* 
* <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> Melon</span>s that grow in the <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> west</span> are round and striped and green and wait a second. That's a <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> watermelon</span>. I guess they come from the western part of Japan.
* The reading for <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [西](https://jisho.org/search/西)</span> here is an exception. Here's a mnemonic to help you remember it:<br /><span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> Watermelon</span>s taste best after you <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> swi</span>ng (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [すい](https://jisho.org/search/すい)</span>) them around. Whether you tie a rope to them or swing them on an actual swing, it mixes up the yummy watermelon insides and they end up tasting the best this way. So always remember to swing your watermelon before you crack it open.
