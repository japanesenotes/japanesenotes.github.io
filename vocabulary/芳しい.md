* 
* This is a single kanji with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [い](https://jisho.org/search/い)</span> on the end, meaning you know it's probably an adjective. What's the adjective form of <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> perfume</span>? <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> Sweet</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> fragrant</span>. 
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn this reading yet, so here's a mnemonic to help you:<br />If you want to smell a <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> sweet</span> fragrance, just open some potpourri. But be careful if it's a can. Fragrant <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> can</span>s will <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> baa</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [かんば](https://jisho.org/search/かんば)</span>) like a sheep at you when you open them. That can baa is just a sweet smell side effect.
