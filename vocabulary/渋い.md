## Mnemonics:

* 
* This is a single kanji with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [い](https://jisho.org/search/い)</span> on the end, meaning you know it's probably an adjective. The adjective form of <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bitter</span> is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bitter</span>, or more specifically, a <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bitter flavor</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: She gives you some food, and it's terribly <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bitter</span>. You look at the lady who gave you this bitter food. You say, <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> She boooo</span> as in, she's no good, and she serves bitter food.
