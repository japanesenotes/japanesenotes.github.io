## Mnemonics:

* 
* This word consists of kanji with hiragana attached. Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> serve</span>, so the verb version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to serve</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to pour</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: <br />What do you decide <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to serve</span> your guests? <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> Coo</span>kies (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [く](https://jisho.org/search/く)</span>)! But not just any cookies. Cookie smoothies! They're delicious and you won't pour anyone anything else. They must drink your delicious cookie drinks.
