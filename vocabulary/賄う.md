## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bribe</span>, but when you pay a bribe, you are going <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to pay</span> something or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to cover</span> something, like a meal, or something like that.
* You need to make a payment, <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> make a</span> payment <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> now</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [まかなう](https://jisho.org/search/まかなう)</span>) <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to cover</span> the cost of this room.
