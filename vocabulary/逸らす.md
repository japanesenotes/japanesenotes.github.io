## Mnemonics:

* 
* This word consists of kanji with hiragana attached. Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> deviate</span>, so the verb version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to turn away</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to avert</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading.  You haven't learned this reading yet, so here's a mnemonic to help you:<br />You want <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to turn away</span> from this movie because it's not worth watching. I mean, it isn't bad, but it isn't good. It's just <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> so</span>-so (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [そ](https://jisho.org/search/そ)</span>). But that's not enough to keep your interest.
