## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> step</span> and the verb vocab version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to step on</span>. So, not quite to step like you might expect, but at least you're stepping still, though it's on something.
* You want <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to step on</span> that bug? You <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> foo</span>l (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [ふ](https://jisho.org/search/ふ)</span>)! That's a killer shoe stabbing bug, it will cut right through your sole and poison you via your foot! <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> Foo</span>l!
