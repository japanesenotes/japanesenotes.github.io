# <bigfont> 出血</bigfont> - Bleeding, Bleed 

| Vocabulary | Meanings | Reading | Characters | Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 出血 | Bleeding, Bleed | しゅっけつ |  [出](../kanjis/出.md) (Exit), [血](../kanjis/血.md) (Blood) | [Level 6](../levels/wk_level6.md) |

Previous vocab: [血](血.md) Next vocab: [国](国.md) 

## Mnemonics:
指を抜いていれば、鼻からの出血は止まると思います。
* I think my nose would stop bleeding if I kept my finger out of it.
* When the thing that is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> exit</span>ing is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> blood</span>, you are probably <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bleeding</span>.
* This is a jukugo word, which usually means on'yomi readings from the kanji. If you know the readings of your kanji you'll know how to read this as well. <br />Note that the <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [しゅつ](https://jisho.org/search/しゅつ)</span> from <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [出](https://jisho.org/search/出)</span> gets shortened to <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [しゅっ](https://jisho.org/search/しゅっ)</span>. That's a small <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [つ](https://jisho.org/search/つ)</span> there, not a big one.
