## Mnemonics:
未来はまだまだ明るいよ、おじいちゃん。
* Your future is still bright, Grandpa.
* This is a single kanji with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [い](https://jisho.org/search/い)</span> on the end, meaning you know it's probably an adjective. What's the adjective form of <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bright</span>? It's also <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bright</span>.
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: <br />You have to remember the <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [あか](https://jisho.org/search/あか)</span> portion. What is <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [あか](https://jisho.org/search/あか)</span> in Japanese? It is red. So, think of something that's really <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> bright</span>. Now, imagine it being totally red (aka <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [あか](https://jisho.org/search/あか)</span>). In Japan, the color red is associated with the sun, and the sun is pretty bright. If you can remember this you can also remember the reading of this word.
