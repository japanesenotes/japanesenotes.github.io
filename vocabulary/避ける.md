# <bigfont> 避ける</bigfont> - To Avoid, To Dodge 

| Vocabulary | Meanings | Reading | Characters | Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 避ける | To Avoid, To Dodge | さける, よける |  [避](../kanjis/避.md) (Dodge, Avoid) | [Level 38](../levels/wk_level38.md) |

Previous vocab: [繰る](繰る.md) Next vocab: [避難](避難.md) 

## Mnemonics:

* 
* Because the hiragana ends with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [う](https://jisho.org/search/う)</span> sound, you know this word is a verb. The kanji itself means <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> avoid</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> dodge</span> and the verb vocab version is <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to avoid</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to dodge</span>!
* Each reading has a slightly different meaning. Here's some mnemonics to help you remember them:<br />You need <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to avoid</span> people who drink <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> sake</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [さけ](https://jisho.org/search/さけ)</span>) all the time. Why? Because when they get drunk you'll have <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> to dodge</span> the <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> yo</span>-yos (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [よ](https://jisho.org/search/よ)</span>) they spin at you. Sake makes you want to toss the ol' yo-yo around. Avoid them or you'll have to get good at dodging!
