## Mnemonics:

* 
* This is a single kanji with an <span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [い](https://jisho.org/search/い)</span> on the end, meaning you know it's probably an adjective. What's the adjective form of <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> gentle</span>? It's <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> gentle</span> (but also <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> kind</span> or <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> warm hearted</span>).
* Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: What do people who are <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> gentle</span> and <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> kind</span> say when someone asks of them a favor? Always <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> yessir</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [やさ](https://jisho.org/search/やさ)</span>). Always, always yessir!
