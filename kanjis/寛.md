# <bigfont> 寛</bigfont> - Tolerance 

| Kanji | Meanings | Reading | Radicals | Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 寛 | Tolerance | かん, くつろ | [宀](../radicals/宀.md) (Roof), [艹](../radicals/艹.md) (Flowers), [見](../radicals/見.md) (See),  | [Level 53](../levels/wk_level53.md) |

Previous kanji: [輔](輔.md) Next kanji: [胞](胞.md) 

## Mnemonics
 * The <span style="background-color:#ADD8E6"> roof</span> <span style="background-color:#ADD8E6"> flowers</span> <span style="background-color:#ADD8E6"> see</span> everything. But, they only see. That is because they learned the art of <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> tolerance</span>.
* These roof flowers only see because they do not judge. They accept everything. We humans can learn a thing or two about tolerance from these roof flowers.
* <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> Tolerance</span> is one thing that Genghis <span style="background-color:#ffcccb"> Khan</span> (<span style="background-color:#fed8b1"> [かん](https://jisho.org/search/かん)</span>) had a surprising amount of.
* You wouldn't expect it, but he actually allowed many types of cultures, religions, and peoples to exist under his reign. The Mongols had an exceptional amount of tolerance for the time.
